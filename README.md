# Orbital Telemetry & Sensor Fusion Pipeline: Modeling Conflict-Driven Infrastructure Decay and Demographic Displacement in Sudan

An end-to-end data engineering and statistical modeling framework that extracts raw photon-level geospatial radiance observations from orbital satellite sensors, fuses them with ground-truth conflict databases, and evaluates time-lagged econometric relationships to model infrastructure failure and civilian migration patterns.

---

## 🔬 Core Methodology & Hypotheses (Revised Evaluation)

This analytical engine leverages high-frequency satellite observation grids to assess structural disruptions across critical administrative zones within Sudan. The research evaluates three core operational frameworks:

### 📑 Research Question
*To what extent can geospatial radiance data serve as a reliable tracking proxy and leading structural indicator for physical infrastructure degradation and macro-level population displacement during active kinetic conflicts?*

### 🧪 Hypothesis Status Matrix
1. **Hypothesis 1: The "Blackout Vector" of Territorial Capture — 🟢 SUPPORTED**
   * **The Concept:** The tactical transition of municipal control between combatant factions triggers an instantaneous, statistically significant structural break in artificial nighttime light radiance rather than a gradual decline, marking immediate grid-infrastructure failure without subsequent short-term recovery.
   * **The Reality:** Highly supported. Time-series extraction demonstrates an abrupt, non-gradual cliff-dive in radiance matching the April 2023 outbreak line. Furthermore, radiance remains deeply suppressed below the pre-war baseline for nearly three years, confirming a prolonged grid failure extending well past a 12-month horizon.
2. **Hypothesis 2: Safe-Haven "Hyper-Urbanization" and Saturation — 🟡 PARTIALLY SUPPORTED**
   * **The Concept:** Forced domestic displacement causes an exponential surge in the artificial radiance footprint of non-conflict sanctuary hubs (e.g., Port Sudan) inversely mirroring the conflict zone, with growth eventually flattening or hitting a ceiling due to structural resource constraints.
   * **The Reality:** Partially supported. The data clearly validates the inverse-mirroring phenomenon: Port Sudan's light footprint escalates dramatically as Khartoum falls into darkness. However, current data does not exhibit an asymptotic curve or flattening ceiling; rather, it reflects heavy volatility and oscillation. The resource saturation claim remains an **open question** awaiting formal curve-fitting and trend deceleration analysis.
3. **Hypothesis 3: Cross-Border Displacement (The Asymmetry Leakage Model) — ⏳ ACTIVE TESTING**
   * **The Concept:** The rate of localized economic and infrastructural decay within active combat theaters is mathematically proportional to the lagged expansion of nighttime radiance metrics directly across adjacent international checkpoints (e.g., cross-border refugee migration into Adré, Chad).

## 📊 Sensor Fusion & Dynamic Signal Processing

### 1. Khartoum Infrastructure Degradation Proxy
By querying the **NOAA VIIRS (Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite)** sensor array across a multi-year timeline (2021–2026), the ingestion pipeline captures a definitive, instantaneous structural shock. Urban radiance abruptly drops from a high baseline directly at the April 2023 war outbreak threshold, indicating an immediate, systemic disruption of centralized power generation stations.
